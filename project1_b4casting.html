B4Casting

B4Casting was created to help aid fishermen both before and during a trip. Variables such as weather readings are available for offline usage as well as having access to personalized saved information.This gives fishermen the ability to fully prepare for their trip as well as never stop learning about their beloved hobby.

Research

To create a user-friendly foundation for my app, I used many methods to gather both qualitative and quantitative data from my research studies.

Problem Statement

Fishing Hobbyists, especially those with little or no experience, need a way to actively track Fish on the coast. They need to be taught/learn how to read and act on weather reports such as currents, storms, daily temperature, etc. to follow the fish.

User Research

After a thorough competitive analysis of both FishBrain and FishAnglr, two of the largest apps on the market, I conducted user interviews to see just what exactly the users wanted in the market.

Testing & Adjustments


With my High- Fidelity Prototypes ready, I started to develop a plan on how to test my new prototype. I started on a test idea and script on how I would like the participants to test the app. I wanted to see how it would perform under specific, but frequent, real life scenarios that will require the app to hold up against scrupulous attention when the user aims to complete a task.

Usability Testing

I held a test with 5 participants to understand if my app presents an appropriate solution to the needs the market presents. I held 4 tasks presented through scenarios to see if they have the desired reactions when navigating through the application. It presented specific problems such as button padding, more familiar icons, a noticeable search bar, problems with spacing, etc.
